# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Preparation

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete preparing for a marathon race while maintaining a vegetarian, nut-free diet:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (130g), Fat (85g), Carbs (420g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), sunflower seeds (25g), and whole milk (240ml).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Greek yogurt (200g, plain) with granola (40g) and blueberries (80g).
    * Protein: 20g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 55g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Lentil and vegetable soup (400ml), whole wheat bread (80g), hummus (30g), and a side salad with mixed greens (150g), chickpeas (80g), cucumber (60g), and olive oil dressing (15ml).
    * Protein: 28g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with peanut butter alternative (tahini, 25g) and honey (15g). Orange (180g).
    * Protein: 12g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 65g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked tofu (180g) with sweet potato (200g), steamed spinach (200g), and quinoa (80g dry weight) drizzled with olive oil (10ml).
    * Protein: 32g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 95g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Whole grain toast (40g) with almond butter alternative (sunflower seed butter, 20g) and a glass of low-fat milk (240ml).
    * Protein: 20g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 25g

This meal plan emphasizes complex carbohydrates for sustained energy during marathon training, plant-based proteins for muscle recovery, and balanced micronutrients to support endurance performance while remaining vegetarian and nut-free.
